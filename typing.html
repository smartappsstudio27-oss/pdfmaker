How to use Smart Typing

Enter your name, choose a time limit, pick Beginner or Advanced mode, and press Start. Type the passage shown on the left exactly as it appears. When you finish one passage, the next one loads automatically until your time runs out or you press Stop.

Finger placement for beginners

Touch typing starts from the home row. Rest your fingers on A S D F (left hand) and J K L ; (right hand), with your thumbs on the space bar. Each finger is responsible for its own column of keys, as shown below.

Beginner mode progression

Beginner mode walks you through the keyboard step by step: home row drills first, then the top row, then a home + top row mix, then the bottom row, then a home + bottom row mix, and finally easy real words. Once you're comfortable, switch to Advanced mode for full paragraphs.

Tips to improve your typing speed

  • Keep your fingers on the home row between keystrokes.
  • Don't look at the keyboard — trust your fingers and look at the screen.
  • Accuracy first, speed second. A clean 40 WPM beats a sloppy 60 WPM.
  • Practice in short, regular sessions rather than one long session occasionally.

Frequently asked questions

What is WPM?

WPM means Words Per Minute. It's calculated as (correct characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed, which is the standard formula used by most typing tests.

How is accuracy calculated?

Accuracy is the percentage of correctly typed characters out of all characters you typed, including mistakes.

Can I change the passage before finishing it?

Yes — use the Next button in the toolbar at any time to skip to a new passage without waiting for time to run out.

Does this work in Marathi?

Yes — use the language dropdown in the toolbar to switch between English and Marathi passages for both Beginner and Advanced modes.
